Understanding Cat Behavior: Why Do Cats Knead?

Published on October 11, 2024 | Author: XXX

If you’re a cat owner, you’ve probably seen your furry friend kneading on soft surfaces, like a blanket, your lap, or even a pillow. This rhythmic action where cats push their paws in and out against an object is both adorable and puzzling. But what exactly is kneading, and why do cats do it?

What Is Kneading?

Kneading is a natural behavior observed in many cats. Often referred to as "making biscuits" due to its resemblance to dough kneading, it’s believed to be a leftover instinct from kittenhood. When kittens nurse, they knead their mother's belly to stimulate milk flow.

Reasons Cats Knead

  • Comfort and Contentment: Many cats knead when they are relaxed or happy. You might notice this behavior when your cat is snuggling in a cozy spot or getting a good petting session.
  • Marking Territory: Cats have scent glands in their paws. When they knead, they may be marking their territory, spreading their unique scent on the object they’re kneading.
  • Preparing a Nest: In the wild, cats knead soft surfaces like grass to make a comfortable nest for sleeping or giving birth.
  • Comfort from Kittenhood: As kneading is closely associated with nursing, it may provide adult cats with a sense of security or nostalgia.

Is It a Sign of Affection?

Yes! When your cat kneads on your lap, it’s often a sign that they feel safe, comfortable, and bonded with you. Consider it a compliment – your cat is showing love and affection by including you in this comforting behavior.

Should You Be Concerned?

In general, kneading is a harmless and normal behavior. However, if your cat becomes too aggressive with their kneading or starts using their claws, you can gently redirect them to a blanket or pillow. Trimmed claws can also help minimize any discomfort.

Conclusion

While kneading might seem odd at first, it’s a comforting and instinctual behavior for cats. Whether they’re showing affection, marking their territory, or reminiscing about their kitten days, it’s one of the many ways your cat expresses themselves.
